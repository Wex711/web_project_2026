Taxonomic information

The pokemon franchise is centered around pokemon, which are creatures of various shapes and sizes that inhabit the world. Every pokemon has 1-2 elemental types, which determine how they grow and what abilities they end up having. The type of a pokemon also influences what moves they can learn. Like real animals, pokemon have a predetermined range of heights and weights which they can fall into. Most pokemon have a set value for these statistics, which should be included in the pokedex page. Every species of pokemon also has a "pokedex entry", which includes details about their personality and mannerisms. These can be found on the pokedex page.

Habitats

Pokemon, just like animals, live in a variety of habitats such as caves, rivers, fields, mountains, etc. These habitats often coincide with the pokemon's elemental type. For example, a water type pokemon may live in a river or ocean while a grass type pokemon may live in a meadow or field. These habitats influence which pokemon you can encounter based on where you are in the pokemon video games. Each pokedex entry has a short list of habitats which its pokemon can be found in.

Evolution

Another aspect of the pokémon franchise which I have not covered so far is pokémon evolution; in the pokémon franchise, once a pokémon has gained enough battle experience it "evolves" into a new pokémon. This process is not like traditional evolution where species develop new traits over time based on natural selection and adaptability, but rather just a change in forms. Not all Pokémon have evolutions; some only have one form. Which pokemon evolve into which should be clear to users based on existing information.
