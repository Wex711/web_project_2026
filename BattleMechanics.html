Pokemon moves and combat

The pokemon game franchise is structured around turn based combat, where different pokemon try to best each other using their abilities and moves. Some moves and types have advantages over others. On this page, I will store lists of all of the moves pokemon can learn with a separate section for each pokemon listing their optimal moveset. I will also list their types. I also plan to include a list of the best moves of each type. When I make my list of pokemon, I will include which of these top moves a pokemon will learn in their pokexdex description.

Pokemon battle mechanics

Each move that a pokemon can learn is assigned an elemental type. Certain elemental types have what are called "type advantages" over each other, where certain elemental types will do more damage if they have a type advantage. An example of this is water type pokemon having a type advantage over fire type pokemon. Most type advantages are rooted in some form of logic like this. In the pokemon games, you can maximize damage against opposing pokemon which you are battling selecting moves that have a type advantage. There also exists a concept called STAB, which stands for "same type attack bonus". What this does is boost the power of the move used by the pokemon if the elemental type of the move used and the elemental type of pokemon are the same. Certain items in the pokemon games can be given to pokemon to increase their STAB damage, and there exists one variant of these items for every type. For example, fire types can boost their attack damage with "charcoal", while grass types can boost their attack damage with a "miracle seed".

Status and technical moves

In the pokemon games, there are more than one type of move; while there is the traditional moves that do elemental damage, there are also moves called status moves. Status moves stop pokemon from operating at their full capacity through status conditions such as paralysis/sleep, which freeze pokemon for a few turns while not allowing them to act, or burn/poison, which do lingering damage to the pokemon inflicted with it every turn. These moves are increase a person's chance of winning any given pokemon battle. There also exist technical moves which increases a pokemons stats such as attack, defense, or speed. These moves can give a pokemon an upper hand in battle if used properly. One move like this is called "swords dance", which boosts the user's attack power by two stages. All stats have 6 potential boosts which they can get from these types of moves, and they max out beyond 6 uses.
