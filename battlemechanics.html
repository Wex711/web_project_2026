Pokémon moves and combat

The pokémon game franchise is structured around turn based combat, where different pokémon try to best each other using their abilities and moves. Some moves and types have advantages over others. On this page, I will store lists of all of the moves pokémon can learn with a separate section for each pokémon listing their optimal moveset. I will also list their types. I also plan to include a list of the best moves of each type. When I make my list of pokémon, I will include which of these top moves a pokémon will learn in their pokédex description.

Pokémon battle mechanics

Each move that a pokémon can learn is assigned an elemental type. Certain elemental types have what are called "type advantages" over each other, where certain elemental types will do more damage if they have a type advantage. An example of this is water type pokémon having a type advantage over fire type pokémon. Most type advantages are rooted in some form of logic like this. In the pokémon games, you can maximize damage against opposing pokémon which you are battling selecting moves that have a type advantage. There also exists a concept called STAB, which stands for "same type attack bonus". What this does is boost the power of the move used by the pokémon if the elemental type of the move used and the elemental type of pokémon are the same. Certain items in the pokémon games can be given to pokémon to increase their STAB damage, and there exists one variant of these items for every type. For example, fire types can boost their attack damage with "charcoal", while grass types can boost their attack damage with a "miracle seed".

Status and technical moves

In the pokémon games, there are more than one type of move; while there is the traditional moves that do elemental damage, there are also moves called status moves. Status moves stop pokémon from operating at their full capacity through status conditions such as paralysis/sleep, which freeze pokémon for a few turns while not allowing them to act, or burn/poison, which do lingering damage to the pokémon inflicted with it every turn. These moves are increase a person's chance of winning any given pokémon battle. There also exist technical moves which increases a pokémons stats such as attack, defense, or speed. These moves can give a pokémon an upper hand in battle if used properly. One move like this is called "swords dance", which boosts the user's attack power by two stages. All stats have 6 potential boosts which they can get from these types of moves, and they max out beyond 6 uses.
