Pokémon moves and combat

The pokémon game franchise is structured around turn based combat, where different pokémon try to best each other using their abilities and moves. Some moves and types have advantages over others. On this page, I will store lists of all of the moves pokémon can learn with a separate section for each pokémon listing their optimal moveset. I will also list their types. I also plan to include a list of the best moves of each type. When I make my list of pokémon, I will include which of these top moves a pokémon will learn in their pokédex description.

Pokémon battle mechanics

This page serves as the primary interface for the virtual Pokédex. It is designed to display and organize detailed information for the original 151 Pokémon in a clear and accessible format. The interface is inspired by the design of the Pokédex as seen in the Pokémon games and anime. To achieve this, structured elements such as tables and lists are used to organize information efficiently. The goal is to create a layout that is both visually intuitive and easy to navigate. Over time, this page may be expanded to include a comprehensive set of data for each Pokémon. This information will include attributes such as type, height, weight, cries, learned moves, evolutionary stages, held items, and additional relevant details.

Status and technical moves

Within the Pokémon franchise, each Pokémon is assigned a unique identification number that reflects its position in the Pokédex. These numbers provide a standardized method for organizing and referencing Pokémon across games and media. Pokémon that are part of the same evolutionary line are typically positioned sequentially, allowing users to easily identify relationships between species. This structured numbering system enhances usability by enabling quick navigation and comparison. In essence, the Pokédex functions as a comprehensive database of Pokémon information. The numbering system plays a critical role in maintaining organization and ensuring that users can efficiently locate and understand the data presented.
